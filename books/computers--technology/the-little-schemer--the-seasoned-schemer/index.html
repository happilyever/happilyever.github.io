The Little Schemer & The Seasoned Schemer

The notion that “thinking about computing is one of the most exciting things the human mind can do” sets both The Little Schemer and The Seasoned Schemer, apart from other books on LISP. Together, these books will open new doors of thought to anyone who wants to find out what computing is really about.

The Little Schemer introduces computing as an extension of arithmetic and algebra. It introduces programs as recursive functions and briefly discusses the limits of what computers can do. The Seasoned Schemer informs the reader about additional dimensions of computing: functions as values, change of state, and exceptional cases.

The Little Schemer and The Seasoned Schemer are popular as textbooks for Scheme courses as well as companion texts for introductory course in Computer Science.
